Students for Equitable Education Database?

Before the coronavirus crisis, projections showed that more than 200 million children would be out of school, and only 60 percent of young people would be completing upper secondary education in 2030. Education systems worldwide have been hit hard and abruptly by the pandemic.

Months of absence from school owing to COVID-19 are likely to impact education outcomes. In the longer term, prolonged absence from school is associated with lower retention and graduation rates and worse learning outcomes, in particular among segments of the population that are already disadvantaged, including members of poor households and students with disabilities.

With the imposed guidelines due to COVID-19, a new method of learning for academic institutions was made which is the online learning setup. Students and teachers now rely on online connections to discuss topics for their classes. Gadgets and online connection became a necessity for students making it an additional burden for those financially challenged students.

This situation gave rise for charitable organizations, private or public institutions, to increase their scope in financially aiding students in need. Various groups are open into helping students to achieve quality education by providing financial help through scholarships and other programs.

Scholarship requirements include official school records confirming their enrollment as students. These documents are protected by academic institutions and will serve as the basis for studentsâ€™ acceptance in a scholarship program.

Through this, Project SEED: Students for Equitable Education Database intends to provide a service which will record information about the students who are financially challenged. This allows charitable organizations to easily reach out to the data about those students needing financial aid,
